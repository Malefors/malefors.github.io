To calculate the current indoor temperature during our simulation we use the physical law:

ΔE = m•c•ΔT


where

ΔE = the energy needed to raise the temperature in the house from our reference temperature 0ºC to the starting temperature for our simulation

m = Mass of the house

c = a weighed avarage of the heat capcities of the materials in the house

and

ΔT = the difference between the initial temperature and our reference temperature 0ºC


If we set

m•c = K1 which is the total heat capacity for the house i. e. the energy needed to raise the temperature in the house 1ºC (compare with the structure of model I)


we can rewrite the first formula above to


ΔE = K1•ΔT


and rearange we get


ΔT = ΔE/K1


Hence, we put in the expression below in the variable In Door Temperature:


[Energy]/[K1]
